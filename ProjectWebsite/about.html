Count Everything

Overview

A key barrier to biomedical data integration is the lack of standard data models and interfaces to operate on these data in a secure and privacy-preserving manner. We show the results of a collaboration between the NIH National Center for Biomedical Computing Integrating Data for Analysis, Anonymization, and SHaring (iDASH), the Big Data to Knowledge (BD2K) Biomedical and Healthcare Data Discovery Index Ecosystem (bioCADDIE), the BD2K Centers of Excellence Mobile-Sensor Data-to-Knowledge (MD2K), Patient-centered Information Commons: Standardized Unification of Research Elements (PIC-SURE), and the Center for Big Data in Translational Genomics (CBDTG). Our system enables researchers to pose a secure and anonymous count query across mobile health data (MD2K), patient data (PIC-SURE) and genomic data (CBDTG), then divides the query into domain-specific parts and sends them to three BD2K centers (MD2K, PIC-SURE, and CBDTG). Each center provides partial results corresponding to their domain; the system uses secure computation models to return results back to the researcher without compromising privacy.
